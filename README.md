## 💡 The Concept 

**NomNom** was developed in a collaborative team environment as a strictly visual, text-minimal educational tool. My focus was on ensuring that the "Counting Hands" and "Feeding the Monster" mechanics were intuitive and accessible for children, bridging pedagogical goals with interactive mechanics.

## 🎨 UX/UI Design & Interaction

While the project involved cross-functional collaboration, my primary role was **UI/UX Design, Interaction Logic, and Sound Integration**. I focused on creating an inclusive environment using **Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)** principles:
* **User-Centered Design**: Developed the interface based on **User Personas** specifically tailored for 1st-grade students to ensure the learning curve was appropriate for their developmental stage.
* **Accessibility & WCAG**: Applied **WCAG Accessibility** principles to create a text-minimal experience, allowing children of different reading levels to navigate the game independently.
* **Interaction Mechanics**: Used **GDScript animations** to provide immediate visual feedback, turning mathematical logic into engaging, "juicy" interactions that reinforce positive learning.
* **Visual Fidelity**: Translated initial wireframes and prototypes into high-fidelity assets, ensuring a consistent and friendly aesthetic throughout the game.
